# Style dimensions and alignment norms

The measurable dimensions `style_signature.py` extracts, what each means, and how to align a draft toward a target without changing substance.

| Dimension | How it's measured | Aligning the draft |
|---|---|---|
| Terminology | Frequency of domain terms + chosen variants ("dataset" vs "data set"; "method" vs "approach" vs "technique") | Adopt the target's preferred variant consistently; build a glossary from the corpus |
| Sentence rhythm | Mean and variance of sentence length (words) | If the draft is choppier/denser than the target, split or merge — never at the cost of a claim |
| Hedging level | Density of hedges (may, might, could, suggests, appears) | Calibrate to the target: under-hedged reads overclaiming; over-hedged reads timid |
| Boosters / claims | Density of boosters (clearly, significantly, novel, first) | Match the target's assertiveness; never inflate a result to do so |
| Connectives | Usage rate of however/moreover/furthermore/thus/therefore | Match cadence; reduce stacking (a `polish-prose` overlap) |
| Voice | Passive-voice rate; first-person ("we propose") frequency | Move toward the target's norm; surface author-vs-venue conflicts rather than guessing |
| Citation density | Citations per 1000 words; integral ("Smith et al. show") vs parenthetical | Match the target's citation cadence and integration style |
| Structure | Section-heading vocabulary; presence/order of conventional sections | Rename/reorder headings toward target conventions (coordinate with `tailor-to-venue`) |
| Contribution framing | Explicit contributions list; "in this paper we" patterns | Adopt the author's/venue's established framing pattern |

## Author voice vs. venue register — resolving conflicts

When the author's habitual style and the venue norm disagree, **present the trade-off; do not silently choose**:

- *First person* — your prior papers may use "we propose" heavily; some venues/sections prefer passive. Default: keep the author's voice unless the venue profile or exemplars clearly require otherwise; flag it.
- *Hedging* — venue exemplars set the safe range; the author's habit is secondary when it would read as overclaiming to that venue's reviewers.
- *Terminology* — venue-standard terms win over personal idiosyncrasy (reviewers expect the field's vocabulary); keep the author's term only if it's an established contribution name.

## Hard rules

- Numbers, results, claims, and citations are immutable. Style alignment is rewording, reordering, and term-normalizing only.
- A measured deviation is a *suggestion*, not a mandate — the author's intentional stylistic choices are theirs to keep. Report the gap; let them decide.
- Never use this to imitate a specific other author's identifiable voice (impersonation) or to defeat AI-use disclosure.
