# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

This project is for researchers, students, maintainers, and builders working
on publication workflows. Discussion should stay useful, respectful, and
focused on improving the package.

Examples of welcome behavior:

- Be kind and specific when giving feedback.
- Assume good intent, especially across fields, languages, and experience
  levels.
- Critique ideas, code, and documentation rather than people.
- Credit prior work and contributors clearly.
- Respect copyright, confidentiality, and private research material.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, insults, threats, or discriminatory comments.
- Publishing someone else's private data, drafts, reviews, credentials, or
  unpublished research artifacts without permission.
- Submitting copyrighted paper content, private benchmark corpora, or generated
  caches that should not be redistributed.
- Repeatedly derailing issues or pull requests after maintainers ask to refocus.

## Scope

This code applies in project spaces, including issues, pull requests,
discussions, reviews, and any other project-related communication.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments; close issues or pull requests;
or block participation when behavior harms the project or its contributors.
Enforcement should be proportionate, documented when appropriate, and focused
on keeping the project safe and useful.

To report a concern, contact the maintainer privately through GitHub. Do not
post sensitive personal, security, or unpublished research details publicly.
